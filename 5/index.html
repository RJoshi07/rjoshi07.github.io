Part 1.6

For 1.6, I repeated the same thing from 1.5, expect this time I implemented denoising with Classifier-Free Guidance, or CFG. For this, I computed the noise estimate, both conditioned on a text-prompt ("a high quality photo") and without a text-prompt, and took something like a weighted average of the 2. There were my results! These images look a lot better in terms of quality and also appear more vibrant.

Part 1.8

For 1.8, I used the same iterative denoising function with cfg as before, but now with a twist. I applied the algorthm to 2 seperate prompts, and for one of the prompts, I called it on the flipped version of an image, and flipped the result of that. Then I took the average of the 2 images. This gave me an image which followed one prompt rightside up and another upside down. The first example was for the prompts "a skyscraper" and "a tall tree". The second for "a sketch of a cup coffee" and "a sketch of an island in the middle of the ocean". I had to run the algorithm as few times to produce good resulrs for these, as to allow the model to train and get better.


Part 1.9

And finally, for 1.9, I used the same algorithm once again. This time, like 1.8, I called it for 2 seperate prompts, but the difference was I combined them both rightside up, just using the high-pass version of one of the images and the low-pass version of the second. To achieve a good balance between both the images, I played around with the size of my kernel for filtering, until I was able to somewhat see both elements of the prompts in the image. The first combines "a cake" and "a hat" and the second combines "a house" with "a painting of a face"

In this section, instead of training denoising noisy digits, I attempted to train for denoising pure noise itself. In this section, I added time-conditioning to my UNet from Part 1. This is similar the iterative denoising from Project 5a, where now our model takes in an image + a timestep the image is at in its denoising process. I also implemented the new training algorithm, which incorporates 2 new FC (fully-connected) blocks that utilize the timestep t.
